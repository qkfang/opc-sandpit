Fun Facts About the Sky

🔵

Why Is the Sky Blue?

Sunlight is made of all colors of the rainbow. When it enters our atmosphere, gas molecules scatter shorter (blue) wavelengths in every direction — so wherever you look, blue light reaches your eye first.

☁️

Clouds Are Mostly Air

A typical cumulus cloud weighs around 500 tonnes — yet it floats effortlessly because its tiny water droplets are spread across an enormous volume of air that is less dense than the surrounding sky.

🌅

Why Sunsets Are Orange & Red

At sunrise and sunset, sunlight travels a much longer path through the atmosphere. Blue and violet light scatter away, leaving the warmer reds, oranges, and pinks to paint the horizon.
